Marketing methodology, funnel design, and execution playbooks for D2C ecommerce brands. Loadable by any agent working on marketing tasks.

## The Marketing Funnel (D2C Ecommerce)

```
AWARENESS → INTEREST → CONSIDERATION → CONVERSION → RETENTION → ADVOCACY
```

| Stage | Goal | Channels | Key Metric |
|-------|------|----------|------------|
| Awareness | They know we exist | Social, SEO, community, word-of-mouth | Reach, impressions |
| Interest | They engage with content | Blog, video, social engagement | Followers, email signups |
| Consideration | They evaluate products | Email flows, product pages, reviews | Email open rate, page views |
| Conversion | They buy | Cart, checkout, discount codes | Conversion rate, AOV |
| Retention | They buy again | Post-purchase flows, loyalty, reorder reminders | Repeat rate, LTV |
| Advocacy | They refer others | Referral programs, UGC, product inserts | NPS, referral revenue |

## Email Marketing Playbook

### Core Flows (implement in this order — these generate 80%+ of automated revenue)

1. **Welcome Series** (3 emails over 7 days)
   - Email 1: Brand story + discount code (10-15%)
   - Email 2: Best sellers / what makes us different
   - Email 3: Social proof + discount expiry reminder
   - Target: 50%+ open rate

2. **Abandoned Cart** (3 emails over 3 days)
   - Email 1: 1 hour after — reminder with product image
   - Email 2: 24 hours — address objections, add social proof
   - Email 3: 48 hours — final reminder, consider sweetener
   - Target: 5-15% recovery rate

3. **Browse Abandonment** (2 emails over 2 days)
   - Email 1: 4 hours — "Still looking at [product]?"
   - Email 2: 24 hours — related products, social proof

4. **Post-Purchase** (3 emails over 14 days)
   - Email 1: Day 1 — thank you + usage tips
   - Email 2: Day 7 — how's it going? + related products
   - Email 3: Day 14 — review request

5. **Winback** (2 emails at 60 and 90 days)
   - Email 1: "We miss you" + what's new
   - Email 2: Incentive to return

### Campaign Best Practices
- Subject lines: 6-10 words, clear value, no clickbait
- Send time: test and optimize per audience
- Frequency: 1-2 campaigns/week max for a small list
- Segmentation: engaged (opened in 90 days) vs dormant — never blast everyone
- A/B test: one variable at a time

## Social Media Playbook

### Content Mix (70/20/10)
- **70%** value content (education, inspiration, behind-the-scenes)
- **20%** shared content (customer UGC, community posts, reposts)
- **10%** promotional (product launches, sales, discount codes)

### Platform Strategy
| Platform | Content Type | Frequency | Best For |
|----------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Instagram | Reels, carousels, stories | 3-5x/week | Brand building, visual storytelling |
| TikTok | Short-form video, trends | 3-5x/week | Reach, younger audience |
| X/Twitter | Thoughts, threads, engagement | Daily | Community, thought leadership |
| Email | Campaigns + flows | 1-2x/week + automated | Conversion, retention |

## Ad Campaign Guardrails

- Never exceed daily budget cap without human approval
- Start new campaigns at minimum viable spend ($5-10/day)
- Scale only after 3+ days of positive ROAS
- Kill underperformers after 5 days of negative ROAS with 1000+ impressions
- Always A/B test (minimum 2 creatives per ad set)
- Use organic content that performs well as ad creative
- Start with lookalike audiences from existing customers
- Exclude existing customers from acquisition campaigns

## Analytics — Key Metrics

| Metric | Source | Healthy Range |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Conversion rate | Shopify | 1.5-3% for D2C |
| Email open rate | Klaviyo | 35-50% (small engaged list) |
| Email click rate | Klaviyo | 3-5% |
| Cart abandonment rate | Shopify | 60-80% (industry norm) |
| Repeat purchase rate | Shopify | >20% is good |
| Social engagement rate | Platform | >3% is strong |
| CAC | Calculated | Must be < 1/3 of LTV |

### Red Flags
- Open rates below 25% → list hygiene problem
- Conversion rate below 1% → product page or pricing issue
- CAC exceeding LTV → stop paid acquisition immediately
- Unsubscribe rate above 0.5% per campaign → frequency or relevance problem

## Values-Based Marketing (Islamic Brand Context)

### Do
- Lead with values alignment (69% of buyers choose based on values)
- Use community networks (mosque partnerships, events, word-of-mouth)
- Show confidence in Islamic identity — differentiator, not limitation
- Use authentic storytelling (founder story, customer stories)
- Include product insert cards with deeper messaging in every order

### Don't
- Use "halal" as a gimmick without substance
- Exploit religious imagery for sales
- Use haram marketing tactics (exploitative imagery, false scarcity)
- Copy mainstream beauty marketing language
- Compromise brand voice for broader appeal
